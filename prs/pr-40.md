## Problem

We want every pull request to get a **technical change-note page** (born at PR open, kept in sync with commits and lifecycle) and every merge-to-main to get a **user-facing release page** — published as their own section of the docs site.

## Approach

- **`pages.py`** — a zero-dependency stdlib generator (sibling to `report.py`). It renders the markdown **records** under `prs/` and `releases/` into `site/public/{prs,releases}/*.html`, reusing the site's real chrome: it mirrors `Base.astro`'s header/footer/logo and emits `pr-pages.css` from the site's own `tokens.css` + `global.css`, so the pages can't drift from the site.
- **Records are the source of truth** and are committed; the generated HTML/CSS is build output (git-ignored, regenerated in CI).
- **`deploy-site.yml`** now regenerates the pages (`python pages.py --refresh`, live commit history from `gh`) before the Astro build, and also triggers on `prs/**`, `releases/**`, `pages.py`.
- **`pr-pages.yml`** (on `pull_request` events) creates/updates the PR's record and commits it to `main` (via the `PAGES_PUSH_TOKEN` PAT, since Pages only publishes from main); on merge it finalizes or scaffolds the release page.

## Notes

- **Bootstrapping:** `pages.py` and the workflows don't exist on `main` until this merges, so `pr-pages.yml` is guarded to **skip** on this PR. After merge, subsequent PRs get pages automatically.
- One-time setup done: `PAGES_PUSH_TOKEN` secret added.
